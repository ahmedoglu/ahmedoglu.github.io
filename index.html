About

Hi there, I'm Mümin Ahmedoğlu a senior student at Middle East Technical University. During my university years, I got a solid background in probability and statistics, operations research, data structures and algorithms, data mining and their applications. With an average of 2 real-world case studies/projects in every course I took at university, I could build a strong understanding of academic knowledge by combining practical and theoretical learning.

When it comes to knowledge I am an insatiable student. I did not stop at the point where the university brings me. I have been trying to hunt more wisdom and more knowledge power. So, I have taken online courses from various universities, companies and online learning platforms including Udacity, Bertelsmann Tech, WorldQuand University, Coursera, MITx and other exciting educational organizations.

I have been working in different positions in the data sector, from data analyst to junior data scientist and now I work as a data engineer at a business intelligence and analytics company in Ankara and I am consulting for an online e-commerce company in their machine learning and analytics department.

When it comes to Data Science, I consider myself as a data nerd. I fall for data. Data is the word that can wake me up in the midnight and get me to forget everything else while I am working on any data-related problem. This word 'Data' is the reason for many sleepless nights working on data course material, writing codes, developing algorithms, modelling data, writing queries or trying to understand a new technology in data science.

Learning & Education

Middle East Technical University, Turkey - Bachelor’s of Industrial Engineering


- INTRODUCTION TO DATA MINING

Introduction to data mining. Data types. Classification Analysis: Rule Based, Nearest-Neighbor and Bayesian Classifiers, Support Vector Machines. Association Analysis: Rule Generation. Cluster Analysis: Center-based Clustering, Hierarchical, Density-based and Fuzzy Clustering. Cluster Validation, Anomaly Detection, Case studies.

- PRODUCTION AND SERVICE INFORMATION SYSTEMS

Analysis and design of information systems with special emphasis given to production sub-systems. Basic information concepts, data processing technology and its applications. Information systems development methodology in terms of systems analysis, design and implementation. The information requirements of production systems in modular form.

- ENGINEERING STATISTICS

Descriptive statistics, statistical estimation, hypothesis testing, simple and multiple linear regression, introductory experimental design with emphasis on industrial engineering applications.

- INTRODUCTION TO PROBABILITY

Elements of probability. Random variables and distributions. Two and higher dimensional random variables. Mathematical expectation. Generating functions. Limiting and sampling distributions.

- SIMULATION

Discrete event simulation methodology and model building. Modeling with a simulation language. Selecting input probability distributions. Random number and random variate generation. Statistical analysis of the simulation output. Basic issues in the design, verification and validation of simulation models.

- STOCHASTIC MODELS IN OPERATIONS RESEARCH

Introduction to stochastic processes. Discrete-time Markov chains. Mean first passage times. Steady-state analysis. Basics of queueing theory. Types of queues. Deterministic and stochastic dynamic programming.

- NETWORK FLOWS AND INTEGER PROGRAMMING

Transportation and network models. Solution algorithms. Project scheduling. Integer programming models. Weak and strong formulations. Cutting plane and branch and bound algorithms. Heuristic approaches to large-scale problems. Introduction to nonlinear programming. Karush-Kuhn-Tucker optimality conditions.

- LINEAR PROGRAMMING

History and methodology of operational research. Introduction to linear programming and graphical solution. Linear programming models and interpretation of solutions. Simplex method, sensitivity analysis and duality. Multi-criteria decision making. Game Theory.

- SCIENTIFIC COMPUTING FOR INDUSTRIAL ENGINEERING

Computer arithmetic. Vectors and matrices. Numerical, symbolic, and graphical capabilities of MATLAB. Execution control and data structures. User-defined and built-in functions. Developing algorithms and programming. Recursion, searching and sorting. Complexity of algorithms and performance analysis. Function approximation, integration, and differentiation by numerical methods. Solution of systems of equations. Graph search and network algorithms. Random number generation and statistical applications. Visualization and plotting of results.

- C PROGRAMMING

C Programming; constants, variables, expressions, statements; selective structures; repetitive structures and arrays; functions and recursive programming; pointers, strings and multi-dimensional arrays; structures and unions; dynamic memory management & linked-lists; bit-wise operations; file processing.
